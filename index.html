About

My research asks how economic statistics and welfare criteria should represent well-being when prices, constraints, and preferences are changing, with a particular interest in how the climate transition will affect inequality.

Research interests

Welfare economics; measurement of well-being, inequality and real income; consumer behavior and preferences; climate transition and living standards.

Well-being Real income Inequality Preferences Climate transition

Current position

PhD candidate in Economics at Paris School of Economics. Dissertation: Preferences and living standards in a greening economy: theory and measurement. Supervisor: Marc Fleurbaey.

Papers

Working paper

Will Standard-of-Living Indicators Capture the Welfare Effects of Climate Transition Policies?

Didier Blanchet and Craig Pesme

Compares standard living-standard indicators with consumer utility under green creative destruction, carbon taxation and quantitative restrictions on carbon-intensive consumption.

A shorter version was published in Economie et Statistique / Economics and Statistics, 543, 3-20, 2024.

PDF Published version DOI
Working paper

Inequality Aversion and the Measurement of Real Income

Craig Pesme

Studies how real income should be cardinalized and how social welfare should be evaluated when reference prices affect the degree of inequality aversion embodied in welfare comparisons.

PDF
Working paper

Fair Interpersonal Comparisons: Characterizing Contour-Set Volume Measures of Well-Being

Craig Pesme

Characterizes volume-type well-being measures for comparing individuals with heterogeneous preferences while avoiding arbitrary interpersonal utility normalizations.
